# BlueOrbit Technologies - IT and Security Policies

## 1. Password Policy
All employees must create passwords that are at least ten (10) characters long and include both letters and numbers.
Passwords must be rotated every 60 days and may not reuse the previous five passwords.
Temporary passwords provided for new hires expire within 24 hours.

## 2. VPN Access Policy
Employees working remotely must connect through the company VPN.
The VPN requires multi-factor authentication (MFA) and automatically disconnects after 20 minutes of inactivity.
All VPN activity is logged and monitored by the IT Security team.

## 3. Access Key Management Policy
Developers and administrators must request access keys through the internal credential system.
Access keys are valid for 45 days and must be rotated automatically using approved scripts.
Keys must never be shared, embedded in source code, or stored in configuration files.

## 4. Incident Response Policy
Any suspected phishing email, malware alert, or unauthorized access must be reported immediately to the Security Operations Center (SOC) at soc@blueorbit.com.
The SOC will triage alerts and initiate an incident response within one (1) hour.
Critical incidents trigger an executive review within 24 hours.

## 5. Data Retention Policy
Operational logs and email archives must be retained for at least 12 months.
Customer transaction data is stored securely in the companyâ€™s data warehouse and backed up daily.
All backups are encrypted using AES-256 encryption standards.

## 6. Physical Security Policy
Access to the data center is restricted to authorized personnel only.
Visitors must be escorted at all times, and security cameras record all entry points 24/7.
Security badges must be visible at all times within restricted zones.

## 7. Acceptable Use Policy
Company devices and networks are to be used for legitimate business purposes only.
Users are prohibited from installing unapproved software, visiting malicious websites, or connecting unauthorized USB devices.
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action.
